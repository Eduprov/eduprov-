CS and CA are both professional courses in the commerce sector and have high career scope in both India and abroad. CA also known as chartered accountant governed by the ICAI Indian chartered accountants of India is an international credential that a student earns by following the CA course and writing exams which are held by the ICAI and is a very certified professional career for anyone those who are interested to set an ensemble in the accounting world. A chartered accountant looks after the books of accounts of an organisation, audit accounts and provides all authentic and legitimate information about the financial record of a company and tracking the shuns and mishaps in the records. It is a remarkable course which implies skilful and technical mind-set to deal with huge numbers in a facile way. The salary packages are tremendously juicy and have made a heavy reputation in the industry with CA being a preferred course.

Whereas CS refers to Company secretary also a professional certified course governed by the ICSI Institute of company secretaries of India. CS is a title for the managerial position in an organisation and not the administrative position as it refers to secretary. Instead CS are attorneys who have MBA's and main intentions lie in backing a managerial position to deal with the scope, responsibility that the company and all the co-workers along with the directors operate within the law and have smooth functioning in an organisation. Every organisation would definitely have a CS and CA.

Both the courses time duration vary from country to country. CA takes more time to complete than CS. as CA takes about 5 years to finish to complete the three level exams and to complete the work requirement according to the experience. Whereas CS takes only about 2-3 years to complete the course and to write the 3 level exams.

Both the courses have their own importance and if any student wants to pursue either of this certification can take up the course depending upon their requirements as both have good career prospects.
